To play the game you input the number you want to use. 

Every turn the attacker gets a Thust, Strike, and Smash card of random power, between 1-9. This power is added to the character's base attack stats. For instance Kumayari has 6 Thrust, 3 Strike, and 1 Smash.
If he draws an 4 Thrust, 5 Strike and 7 Smash, he will have 10 Thrust, 8 Strike and 8 Smash. 

The defender likewise gets 1 of each defensive card. Unlike attack cards, characters lack defensive stats. This may change in the future, though not likely.
Unless the defender select the card to counter the attacker's card, the defender will take a minimum of 1 damage. This becomes 10 damage if the attacker wins the matchup.

Thrust beats Block, loses to Parry.
Strike beats Dodge, loses to Block.
Smash beats Parry, loses to Dodge.

The computer, as of now, selects a card entirely randomly. This will change later as I experiment and figure out how difficult the computer should be. 
In the full game, I plan for different characters to perform differently. 
For instance a character like Sir Frederick, who is a seasoned warrior, will be far more intelligent in battle than a character who is a novice. 
This can also be adjusted based on how difficult the fight should be.
A major boss fight will probably outright cheat to some extent, for example, seeing the card you choose and countering it a percentage of the time. 

Hopefully you find this at least a bit amusing to play with.
